Chicken Fried Rice

prep time: 15 MINS | cook time: 35 MINS

total time: 50 MINS | yield: 6 SERVINGS

DIFFICULTY
TAGS:
#LUNCH #SAVORY

INGREDIENTS

  • 1½ cup basmati rice
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 lb boneless chicken breast
  • 2 large eggs
  • 3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 tsp oil
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • ⅓ cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup frozen corn kernels
  • Green onion, to taste
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a casserole dish, combine the rice and 2 cups of water. Cook in the oven at 400°F for 25-30 minutes or until the rice is fluffy. Fluff the rice with a fork and set it aside to cool.
  2. In a skillet, heat 1 teaspoon of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Cook the chicken pieces until they are golden and fully cooked. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Remove the chicken from the pan and set it aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add 1 tablespoon of butter and the frozen corn. Sauté over medium heat until the corn is cooked. Remove the corn from the pan and set it aside with the cooked chicken.
  4. In the same skillet, add another tablespoon of butter and scramble the eggs, stirring as needed. Once the eggs are cooked, add back the chicken and corn along with the minced garlic. Stir to incorporate everything.
  5. Add the cooled rice to the skillet and mix well with the other ingredients.
  6. In a small bowl, combine the ingredients for the sauce: soy sauce, oyster sauce, hoisin sauce, rice vinegar, sesame oil, and red pepper flakes (if using). Pour the sauce into the skillet and add sliced green onions. Fry for another 2-3 minutes, stirring as needed, until everything is well coated in the sauce.
Spicy Kick

For those who enjoy a bit of heat, add some crushed red pepper flakes or a drizzle of sriracha sauce to the fried rice. This will give the dish a spicy kick and add a layer of complexity to the flavors. Adjust the amount of spice according to your preference.

Pineapple Delight

Add diced pineapple to the fried rice for a tropical twist. The sweetness of the pineapple pairs beautifully with the savory flavors of the dish, creating a delightful balance. You can use fresh or canned pineapple, depending on what's available.
