Stuffed Bell Peppers

prep time: 10 MINS | cook time: 1 HR 5 MINS

total time: 1 HR 15 MINS | yield: 3 SERVINGS

DIFFICULTY
TAGS:
#LUNCH #SAVORY #SUMMER

INGREDIENTS

  • 6 medium bell peppers
  • 1 cup millet
  • 1 medium onion, diced
  • 1 garlic clove, minced
  • 1 red chili, minced
  • 1 celery stalk, diced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • ½ tsp ground cumin seeds
  • 2 cups water or vegetable stock
  • Salt to taste

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat your oven to 350°F. Rinse the millet and place it on paper towels to remove excess moisture.
  2. In a pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and sauté until soft and translucent.
  3. Stir in the minced garlic and chili pepper and cook for about a minute. Add the millet to the pan and stir for a few minutes until it's lightly toasted.
  4. Add the diced celery, canned tomatoes, tomato paste, and cumin seeds. Cook for another minute. Pour in the vegetable stock and add the salt. Stir everything together and bring it to a boil. Then, reduce the heat to low, cover the pan, and let it cook for 15 minutes.
  5. While the millet is cooking, prepare the bell peppers. When the millet is done cooking, switch off the heat and let it sit with the lid on for another 5 minutes. Fluff the millet with a fork and stuff each bell pepper with the millet mixture. Cover each pepper with its "lid" and place them in a slightly greased baking dish.
  6. Bake the stuffed peppers in the preheated oven for 40 minutes.
Cheese It Up

Before baking the stuffed peppers, sprinkle some grated cheese, such as cheddar, mozzarella, or feta, on top of each pepper. The cheese will melt and create a delicious golden crust that adds richness to the dish.

Mediterranean

Enhance the flavors by adding Mediterranean ingredients. Mix in some chopped Kalamata olives, sun-dried tomatoes, and crumbled feta cheese into the millet mixture before stuffing the peppers. These additions will bring a burst of tangy and savory taste to the dish.
