Croissants

prep time: 90 MINS | cook time: 30-40 MINS | additional time: 12 HRS

total time: 14 HRS | yield: 10 CROISSANTS

DIFFICULTY
TAGS:
#BREAKFAST #SAVORY #FALL

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup + 2 tbsp milk, room temperature
  • 2½ tsp active dry yeast
  • 2¼ cups unbleached all-purpose flour
  • ⅔ cup bread flour
  • 2 tsp kosher salt
  • ¼ cup sugar
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter, softened
  • 1 cup unsalted butter, cold
  • 1 egg

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. In a bowl or stand mixer, mix the milk and yeast until dissolved. Add all-purpose flour, bread flour (if using), salt, sugar, and 2 tablespoons soft butter. Stir until fully combined and the dough is smooth. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 6 hours or up to 12 hours to allow the dough to rise.
  2. After at least 6 hours, place the dough on a floured surface, forming an 8-inch square with triangular flaps at the corners.
  3. Roll out each flap into a square shape, creating a 12-inch-wide dough with a 6-inch lump in the center and squarish flaps on each side.
  4. Place the cold butter in the mixer and beat on medium speed to soften it. Spread the softened butter on the 6-inch lump in the center of the dough.
  5. Fold the flaps over the butter to cover it entirely. Press down on the dough to create an 8-inch square, then roll it into an 18 by 10-inch rectangle. Fold the dough into thirds and refrigerate for 1½ to 3 hours.
  6. Roll the dough into another 18 by 8-inch rectangle, fold it into thirds, and refrigerate for at least 4 hours or up to 16 hours.
  7. Roll the dough into a 30 by 6 to 7-inch rectangle. Cut it into 10 triangles and roll each triangle into a 10 to 12-inch length.
  8. Shape the triangles into croissants and place them on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper. Leave them in a warm place to rise for 2 to 2½ hours.
  9. Preheat the oven to 400°F. Brush the croissants with beaten egg and bake for 5 minutes. Then lower the temperature to 350°F and bake for an additional 25 to 35 minutes until golden brown.
  10. Let the croissants cool on a wire rack for 30 to 40 minutes before serving. Enjoy warm or refresh in a 300°F oven for 5 to 8 minutes before serving the next day.
SAVORY
Ham + Cheese

Place a slice of your favorite ham and a slice of cheese (such as Swiss, cheddar, or Gruyere) on the dough triangles before rolling them up. The combination of savory ham and melty cheese will create delicious ham and cheese croissants.

Spinach + Feta

Sauté fresh spinach with a little garlic until wilted, then add crumbled feta cheese to the mixture. Spread the spinach and feta mixture onto the dough triangles before rolling them up. These spinach and feta croissants will be packed with savory flavors.

Pesto + Sun-Dried Tomato

Spread a layer of basil pesto on the dough triangles, then add a few pieces of chopped sun-dried tomatoes and some grated Parmesan cheese. Roll up the triangles to make delectable pesto and sun-dried tomato croissants.

SWEET
Cinnamon Sugar

Mix together cinnamon and granulated sugar, and sprinkle the mixture over the dough triangles before rolling them up. The warm and fragrant cinnamon sugar croissants will be a delightful treat.

Chocolate Filling

Add a delicious chocolate filling to the croissants for a delightful twist. Spread a layer of chocolate hazelnut spread or melted chocolate over the dough triangles before rolling them up. The warm and gooey chocolate filling will make your croissants irresistible.

Raspberry Jam

Add a spoonful of raspberry jam to the dough triangles before rolling them up. The sweet and tangy raspberry jam will create irresistible raspberry croissants.
