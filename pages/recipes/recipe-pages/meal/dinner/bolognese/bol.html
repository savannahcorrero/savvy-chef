Spaghetti Bolognese

prep time: 10 MINS | cook time: 30 MINS

total time: 40 MINS | yield: 5 SERVINGS

DIFFICULTY
TAGS:
#DINNER #SAVORY #WINTER

INGREDIENTS

  • 1½ tbsp olive oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • ½ cup dry red wine
  • 2 beef bouillon cubes
  • 28 oz can crushed tomato
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tsp white sugar
  • 2 tsp worcestershire sauce
  • 2 dried bay leaves
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • Salt and pepper
  • 13 oz spaghetti, dried
  • Parmesan cheese

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or deep skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and garlic, and cook for 3 minutes or until they become light golden and softened. Increase the heat to high, and add the beef. Cook while breaking it up until it's browned.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook following the packet directions. While the pasta cooks, scoop out a mug of pasta cooking water and set it aside. Drain the pasta once it's cooked.
  3. Pour in the red wine and bring it to a simmer. Cook for 1 minute, scraping the bottom of the pot to remove the alcohol smell.
  4. Add all remaining ingredients except salt and pepper. Stir well, bring to a simmer, then reduce the heat to medium so it gently bubbles. Cook for 20 - 30 minutes without a lid, stirring occasionally. Add water if the sauce becomes too thick for your liking.
  5. Add the pasta into the Bolognese sauce over medium heat, along with about 1/2 cup (125 ml) of reserved pasta water. Gently toss for 1 1/2 - 2 minutes, or until the spaghetti turns red and the sauce thickens.
  6. Divide the spaghetti between bowls and garnish with parmesan and parsley. Enjoy!
Fresh Herbs

Add a handful of fresh herbs, such as basil, oregano, or thyme, to the Bolognese sauce while it simmers. The herbs will infuse the sauce with aromatic flavors and brighten up the dish.

Wine Pairing

Serve the Bolognese spaghetti with a glass of red wine that complements the flavors of the dish. A bold and robust red wine like Chianti or Cabernet Sauvignon can enhance the overall dining experience and add a touch of elegance to the meal.
