Spinach Pizza

prep time: 15 MINS | cook time: 12 MINS

total time: 30 MINS | yield: 8 SERVINGS

DIFFICULTY

TAGS:
#DINNER #SAVORY

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 1 small garlic clove, thinly sliced
  • 2 tsp Greek seasoning
  • ⅛ tsp table salt
  • ⅛ tsp black pepper
  • 10 cups baby spinach
  • 1 pound prepared pizza dough, whole grain if desired, at room temperature
  • ½ cup alfredo sauce
  • ¾ cup mozzarella cheese or fontina cheese
  • 4 ounces crumbled feta cheese
  • Pinch red pepper flakes

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat the oven to 500°F and position the rack in the center. If using a pizza stone, preheat it for at least 30 minutes. In a large non-stick skillet over medium heat, heat olive oil and sauté minced garlic until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  2. Add baby spinach in batches, along with Greek seasoning (if using), salt, and pepper. Cook until the spinach wilts and any excess water evaporates, about 3 minutes. Transfer the cooked spinach to a paper towel-lined plate to cool slightly.
  3. While the spinach cools, roll or shape the pizza dough into a 12-inch circle on a lightly floured surface. Transfer the dough to a parchment paper-lined pizza peel, cutting board, or flat cookie sheet. Alternatively, place the dough on a lightly oiled pizza pan or large baking sheet. Create a 1-inch edge around the crust by pressing outward with one hand and inward with the other.
  4. Evenly spread the Alfredo sauce over the dough, leaving a 1-inch border. Sprinkle the reserved spinach over the Alfredo sauce, followed by the shredded mozzarella or fontina cheese, and crumbled feta. Add a pinch of red pepper flakes for some heat.
  5. Slide the pizza (with the parchment paper) directly onto the oven rack or preheated pizza stone. If using a pizza pan or baking sheet, place the pizza in the center of the oven. Bake the pizza for 10 to 12 minutes or until the edges are golden and the cheese is melted. Slice and enjoy!
Herb Infusion

Infuse the Alfredo sauce with fresh herbs to elevate the taste. Stir in some chopped fresh basil, oregano, or thyme into the Alfredo sauce before spreading it on the dough. The herbs will impart a wonderful fragrance and a delicious herbal note to the pizza.

Roasted Garlic Drizzle

Instead of sautéing the garlic, roast a whole head of garlic in the oven until it becomes soft and caramelized. Mash the roasted garlic cloves and mix them into the Alfredo sauce. The roasted garlic will add a rich and sweet flavor to the pizza, elevating its taste and aroma.
