Steak Fries

prep time: 10 MINS | cook time: 50 MINS

total time: 1 HR | yield: 4 SERVINGS

DIFFICULTY
TAGS:
#DINNER #SAVORY

INGREDIENTS

  • 8 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil
  • 4 large russet potatoes
  • 3 tsp kosher salt
  • 2 tsp white vinegar
  • 3 tbsp cornstarch
  • ½ tsp baking powder

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F and position the racks in the center and lower levels. Brush two large baking sheets with oil.
  2. Place the potatoes, salt, and vinegar in a large pot. Cover with cold water and set the pot over medium heat. Bring it to a boil and cook until the potatoes are semi-translucent and knife-tender, approximately 8 mins. Drain the potatoes and gently pat them dry with paper towels.
  3. In a large bowl, toss the potatoes with the remaining oil, salt, cornstarch, baking powder, and spices until they are evenly coated. Divide the potatoes evenly between the prepared baking sheets and spread them into a single layer.
  4. Bake the potatoes until they become deeply golden, flipping both the fries and the sheet trays positions halfway through the baking time, about 30 to 35 minutes.
Cheesy Potato Fries

After baking the potatoes until deeply golden, sprinkle shredded cheddar or your favorite cheese over the top. Return the baking sheets to the oven for a few minutes until the cheese melts and becomes bubbly, creating delicious cheesy potato fries.

Spicy Fries

For a kick of heat, mix in some cayenne pepper, paprika, or chili powder to the spice blend. The spicy seasoning will add a delightful zing to the potato fries, making them a hit with spice lovers.
