Steak Tacos

prep time: 2 HRS | cook time: 10 MINS

total time: 2 HRS 10 MINS | yield: 5 SERVINGS

DIFFICULTY

TAGS:
#DINNER #SAVORY

INGREDIENTS

  • 1½ pounds flank steak
  • 4 cloves garlic , minced
  • ½ cup fresh chopped cilantro
  • ½ cup onion, chopped
  • 1 tsp chili powder
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • ⅓ cup olive oil
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar
  • 2 limes, juiced
  • ½ of an orange, juiced

INSTRUCTIONS

  1. Season the steak on both sides with salt and pepper. In a mixing bowl, stir together all the marinade ingredients. Place the flank steak in a bowl or large ziplock bag, add the marinade, and sliced onions. Seal/cover and refrigerate, allowing the steak to marinate for 2-5 hours. Remove the steak from the refrigerator 20 minutes before grilling.
  2. Heat the grill to high and brush the grill grates lightly with oil. Lift the steak from the bag of marinade, allowing excess marinade to drip off. Place the steak on the grill and cook for about 3-5 minutes per side, or until desired doneness (depending on whether you like yours medium-rare or well-done).
  3. While the steak is grilling, prepare the pico de gallo by mixing diced tomatoes, red onions, chopped cilantro, lime juice, salt, and pepper in a bowl.
  4. Once the steak is cooked, transfer it to a cutting board, cover with aluminum foil, and allow it to rest for 5 minutes. Cut the steak, against the grain, into very thin strips. Warm the corn tortillas on a hot dry skillet.
  5. Assemble the tacos by placing the sliced steak, pico de gallo, sliced avocado, and optional hot sauce on warm corn tortillas. Add some sautéed onions to the tacos for extra flavor and texture.
Chipotle Lime Crema

Prepare a creamy chipotle lime crema to drizzle over the tacos. Mix together sour cream or Greek yogurt with chipotle pepper in adobo sauce, lime juice, and a pinch of salt. This tangy and slightly spicy sauce will add a delightful kick to the tacos, enhancing the flavors of the grilled steak and fresh toppings.

Grilled Peppers + Corn

Enhance the taco filling by grilling some bell peppers and corn alongside the steak. Toss the sliced bell peppers and fresh corn kernels with a little olive oil, salt, and pepper, then grill until lightly charred and tender. Add the grilled peppers and corn to the taco filling for extra color, sweetness, and a delightful smoky taste.
